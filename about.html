About Us

Highlighting Youth in Journalism

The Youth Press Project connects Baltimore students with journalism workshops, press rights education, and resources to launch their own youth-led news organizations. We're empowering youth to cover their communities through their voices.

Our Mission

At the Youth Press Project, we believe that young people have powerful stories to tell and deserve the tools, knowledge, and platform to share them. We're dedicated to breaking down barriers that prevent students from engaging in journalism and creating their own media outlets.

Our Motivation

Traditional media often overlooks the voices and perspectives of young people, especially those from underserved communities. We founded the Youth Press Project to address this gap by providing Baltimore students with comprehensive journalism training, legal education about their press rights, and ongoing mentorship to help them build sustainable student news organizations. We're motivated by the belief that when young people have the power to tell their own stories, they can create meaningful change in their communities and beyond.

What We Do

Through our free workshops, we teach students the fundamentals of reporting, writing, visual storytelling, and podcasting. We provide critical education about student press freedom laws in Maryland, ensuring young journalists understand their constitutional protections. Our mentorship program connects students with professional journalists and youth organizers who guide them through every step of launching and sustaining their own media outlets. We're not just teaching journalism—we're building a movement of empowered young storytellers.
